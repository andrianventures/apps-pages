Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Dmitriy Andrianov (“we,” “us,” “our”), based in the United States, handles information when you use our mobile applications in the Currency Conversion family of apps (each, an “App,” together, the “Apps”). By using an App, you agree to this policy together with our Terms of Use. Capitalized words used here have the same meaning as in the Terms unless we define them differently below.

We design the Apps to work with minimal personal data. We do not sell your personal information. What we process depends on which App you use and which features you choose.

Information we process

Local data on your device. Apps may store items such as preferences, recent conversion inputs, history lists, and cached exchange rates using your device’s storage. Unless a feature explicitly syncs to a server (we do not operate a general login system for these Apps), that information stays on the device until you delete the App or clear data.

Exchange rates. To show current conversions, an App may request rate data from independent providers over the internet. Those requests are ordinary HTTPS traffic; the provider may receive technical data such as IP address, date/time, and request metadata under its own policies.

Optional image analysis. In Apps that offer scanning or similar features, if you take or select a picture, the image may be sent to a cloud-based analysis service so a numeric amount can be extracted for the calculator. Only use this for images you are allowed to share. Processing is governed by that provider’s terms and privacy notice.

Purchases. If you buy a subscription or other in-app item, the payment platform (for example, Apple) processes payment. We use service providers such as RevenueCat to validate entitlements and manage premium access. Those providers receive transaction-related identifiers and similar data needed to operate purchases.

Support email. If you email us, we receive your address, message content, and metadata your mail system sends (for example, time stamps).

Third-party services

The Apps rely on services we do not control. Their practices may change; review their policies periodically. Examples that may apply, depending on the App and platform:

  • Apple Inc. — App Store, in-app purchases, device permissions. Apple Privacy Policy
  • RevenueCat — subscription and entitlement management. RevenueCat Privacy
  • Google — where an App uses Google cloud AI to interpret price-tag images. Google Privacy Policy
  • Exchange rate data — for example, public APIs such as the Frankfurter project for reference rates. Frankfurter (see their site for technical and policy details).

If we add analytics, advertising, or other tools in a future release, we will update this page and, where required, in-app notices.

Diagnostics and log-type data

When something goes wrong, your device or third-party libraries included in the App may generate diagnostic information (for example device model, OS version, app version, crash logs). If that data leaves your device, it is typically processed under the relevant platform or SDK provider’s policies. We use this category of information to keep the Apps reliable, not to identify you for unrelated marketing.

Cookies and similar technologies (web)

If you use a web-based build of an App or visit this documentation site, your browser may store cookies or similar data as described by the host (for example, GitHub Pages). Mobile App Store builds do not use browser cookies in the same way a website does.

Security

We rely on industry-standard transport encryption where data is sent over the internet and on reasonable safeguards through our service providers. No electronic storage or transmission is completely secure; we cannot promise absolute security.

Children’s privacy

The Apps are not intended for children under 13 in the United States, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have received such information, contact us and we will take appropriate steps to delete it.

Links

The Apps or this site may reference third-party websites. We are not responsible for their content or privacy practices. Read their policies before you use them.

U.S. state privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, delete, or opt out of certain processing of personal information. To exercise rights that apply to information we hold directly, email us. For purchases and device settings, Apple and other platforms may be the primary contact.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will post the new version on this page and revise the “Last updated” date. If a change materially affects how we treat information we collected from you, we will provide notice as required by law or through the App where practical.
